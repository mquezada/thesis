\begin{abstract} 

    %\todo[inline]{revisar el abstract}
    Online activity on social media involves the consumption and production of
    news event-related content. 
    %
    There are about 500 million Twitter messages published every day, and
    according to surveys, 59\% of its users use the platform as a way to get the
    news. 
    %
    Its high rate of production of multimodal content (text, images, and videos)
    necessitates having flexible models to understand the dynamics of the
    information disseminated on social media. 
    %
    This thesis proposes the creation of context models from user-generated
    messages on Twitter to discover knowledge as a way to perform high-level
    quantitative analysis of news events. 
    %
    The proposed models are useful in three perspectives: the activity of users
    that react when an event happens, the spatio-temporal context in which the
    events develop, and the content aggregations that can be exploited to
    generate a comprehensive representation of the event. 
    %
    For this work, we collected a dataset of news-related tweets dating from
    2013 to 2015, encompassing twenty thousand events, and almost two hundred
    million tweets.
    %
    The first perspective concerns the study of what characteristics of news
    events disseminated in social media provoke high levels of activity of its
    users, showing that it is possible to identify with high precision these
    kinds of events.
    %
    Then, the development of a spatio-temporal context representation of events
    allows us to characterize geopolitical entities (e.g., countries) according
    to the news, and we observe that we can study international relations based
    on the news discussed on social media.
    %
    Finally, we propose an event representation based on common shared content,
    such as URLs: this representation allows us to represent sub-topics, or
    aspects, of the event by aggregating posts, and this process is
    substantially faster than using individual posts, and this process yields
    similar quality metrics.
    %
    These different event representations are useful, novel, and effective to
    perform high-level quantitative analysis of news events.

\end{abstract}
